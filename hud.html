Governance H

Main Thesis: Social media platforms must ensure fairness and equality in their advertising tools to prevent discrimination based on race, religion, or other protected characteristics.

  • Discrimination in housing advertising can be as harmful as discrimination in person.
  • Algorithms used by tech companies can unintentionally perpetuate bias and discrimination.
  • Should social media platforms be held responsible for the discriminatory actions of their advertisers?
  • How can technology be used to promote fairness and equality in housing opportunities?
  • What safeguards should be put in place to prevent discrimination in online advertising?
  • Should users have more control over how their data is used for targeted advertising?
  • Are there ethical considerations in allowing advertisers to target specific demographics on social media platforms?
  • How can we ensure that everyone has equal access to housing opportunities, both online and offline?
  • Should there be stricter regulations on how companies collect and use personal data for advertising purposes?
  • Can technology truly eliminate bias in advertising, or will human oversight always be necessary?
  • How do our online actions, such as liking certain pages or posts, influence the ads we see?
  • Should companies like Facebook be transparent about how their algorithms work and how they impact users?
  • What role should government agencies play in regulating online advertising to prevent discrimination?
  • How can we raise awareness about the importance of fair housing practices in the digital age?
  • Social media platforms like Facebook must ensure their advertising tools do not discriminate based on race, religion, or other protected characteristics.
